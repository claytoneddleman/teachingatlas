Executive Summary

The data, the Phoenix peer set, and ten moves worth making.

Central is running the right model for the Phoenix metro: a topical-frame / expository-spine hybrid with strong identity-of-Jesus and grace-themed series tuned to the East Valley LDS-adjacent context. That model lines up cleanly with the dominant peer pattern (CCV, Sun Valley, Compass).

What the Phoenix peer landscape adds: the right peer comparison is not North Point and Life.Church. It's CCV, Sun Valley, Radiant, and Highlands Scottsdale. Three specific Phoenix-market gaps show up only when the comparison is regional.

The four gaps that matter for 2026-27: (1) Central has no clear summer hook while Sun Valley, CCV, Radiant, and Compass all run an annual summer franchise. (2) Central plants no flag in extended Bible-book territory, which is exactly where Highlands and Radiant differentiate from CCV. (3) Central does not yet operate a multi-voice teaching team, which Sun Valley and CCV both use to sustain senior leaders and develop successors, an especially live conversation post Cal-to-Shan. (4) Central has no January Pray First / 21 Days corporate participation engine, which Highlands, Watermark, and Transformation Church have made standard.

Five Central strengths to protect: the "I Am" / identity-of-Jesus brand (East Valley wedge CCV cannot replicate), the sustained mental health track record (deeper than any peer), the Love Beyond / MOHI mission emphasis (deepest competitive moat), the Phoenix-metro annual calendar conformity, and the grace-themed series tuned to LDS context ("Handle With Care," "Flip the Script").

Section 06 contains a concrete 2026-27 calendar that plugs the four gaps into the existing rhythm. Section 07 ranks the ten recommended moves in priority order, with the multi-voice teaching team as the strategic anchor.
